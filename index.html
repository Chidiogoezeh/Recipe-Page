Isi Ewu Recipe

Isi ewu, a popular Nigerian delicacy, requires a variety of ingredients to achieve its unique flavor.
Key components include a goat head, palm oil, potash, calabash nutmeg (ehuru), crayfish, utazi leaves, onions, pepper, and seasoning cubes.
This is consumed as a special dish prepaared using all parts of a goat head, including the brain.
It is favored because of it's high nutritional content and it's savory flavors, when properly prepared.
Caution should also be applied during preparationa and proper sanitation standards are to be adopted to avoid incidences of food posoining.

Recipe Information

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 30 minutes
  • Servings: Considerate quantity
  • Preparation Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • Goat head (I full goat head)
  • Onions (2 big balls)
  • Salt
  • Habanero Pepper
  • All_purpose seasoning (Maggi cubes)
  • Goat brain (Extracted from the goat head)
  • Potash or baking soda or palm ash (Quarter kg)
  • Palm Oil (Quarter liter)
  • Vegatble (Utazi Leaves)
  • Calabash nutmeg (10 to 15 pieces)

Additional Prep Information

  • Cut your goat head meat to bite chunk sizes.
  • Properly and thoroughly wash your goat meat with clean water.
  • Cut Onions into thin rings and tiny pieces, set aside.
  • Slice vegetable (long strings and tiny pieces), set aside.
  • Grind calabash nutmeg, set aside.
  • Add some edible potash to a bowl of water, stir thoroughly to dissolve, strain in a bag to remove unwanted particles.

Cooking Instruction

  1. Put a thoroughly cleaned and cut pieces of goat head in a pot;
  2. Add some cut onions, salt, all-purpose seasoning;
  3. Put the brain separately in a foil paper inside the pot (Helps in sauce thickening);
  4. Add water, cover the pot, set on top of your stove, with heat to medium to high;
  5. Remove the brain after 15 mins, continue cooking the meat;
  6. In a mortar, pound some peppers, add the goat brain, initially cooked, pound together, and set aside;
  7. After 30 minutes, remove the properly cooked meat from the fire;
  8. In another pan, add some palm oil, gradually add your potash mixture to form a thick paste;
  9. Add the pounded pepper and brain mixture;
  10. Add the grinded calabash nutmeg;
  11. Add the tiny pieces cut vegetable (add extra if required);
  12. Add the properly cooked goat head pieces, and mix thoroughly until it is well combined;
  13. Heat it up for about a minute (not compulsory);
  14. Serve in a plate or calabash, garnish with the thin sliced onions and vegetable;
